Introduction to the Digital Revolution

What is the Internet?

The Internet is a global network of interconnected computers and devices that communicate using standardized protocols. It enables the sharing of information, services, and resources across the world.

Key Concept

The Internet is often called the "network of networks" because it connects millions of private, public, academic, business, and government networks.

Why Understanding History Matters

Understanding the history of the Internet and web development provides context for modern tools and frameworks. It also highlights the iterative nature of technology, showing how past innovations and failures have shaped today's digital landscape.

Technical Benefits

  • Understand why certain design patterns exist
  • Learn from past architectural decisions
  • Appreciate the evolution of programming paradigms

Conceptual Benefits

  • See technology as an evolving process
  • Recognize recurring patterns in innovation
  • Develop better problem-solving perspectives

Historical Insight

The first message ever sent over ARPANET (precursor to the Internet) in 1969 was "LO" - an attempt to send "LOGIN" that crashed the system after two characters.
